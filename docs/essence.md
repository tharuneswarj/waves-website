## 1. What Waves Is

Waves Company is a design and manufacturing studio creating algorithmically crafted lighting objects and sculptural installations.

We work at the intersection of computational design, digital fabrication, and material exploration. Every object we make is the result of deliberate thinking — structurally, aesthetically, and technically.

3D printing is our medium. Not our shortcut.

We write custom toolpaths. We manipulate extrusion logic. We use Grasshopper to generate G-code that most printers never receive. The result is objects whose surface, texture, and light behaviour are designed — not defaulted.

---

## 2. Why Waves Exists

Most 3D printed lighting looks like 3D printed lighting. Vase mode. Simple extrusions. Forms chosen for printability rather than purpose.

That gap — between what digital fabrication is capable of and what most people do with it — is where Waves operates.

We believe objects deserve the same intellectual rigour as buildings. That light is not decoration but atmosphere. That a well-considered lamp changes how a room feels in ways that are difficult to articulate but impossible to miss.

Technology expands the design vocabulary.

Judgment decides what to make with it.

---

## 3. How We Work

Each piece is developed through:

- Parametric and computational modeling in Grasshopper
- Custom toolpath strategies and print logic
- Structural validation across iterations
- Iterative prototyping — often many rounds before a form feels resolved
- Hand finishing and careful assembly
- Material selection specific to the light behaviour required

Our forms are not decorative gestures. They are resolved systems.

Layer height influences diffusion. Wall thickness shapes shadow density. Toolpath direction defines surface texture. Every variable is a design decision.

## 4. What We Make

**Table lamps** — the starting point. Designed to live in a room, not perform in one.

**Pendant configurations** — single pendants and clustered installations. Adapted to the spatial brief.

**Wall and floor lamps** — in development. Added to the catalogue as real projects demand them.

**Custom installations** — designed specifically for a space, in collaboration with the architect or designer specifying them.

The catalogue grows from real work. Every project that teaches us something new becomes a product that didn't exist before.

## 5. Foundational Belief

Light shapes how spaces feel.

It influences perception, mood, and rhythm. When designed with intention, light becomes a space's quietest and most powerful element.

We believe the object that carries the light deserves the same attention as the light itself. Proportion matters. Texture matters. How shadow falls matters. How the piece sits in a room when it is switched off matters.

We design with computation.

We decide with judgment.

---

## 6. Design Language

**Form**

Geometries shaped by mathematical logic and iterative refinement. Surfaces reveal their making — visible layers express process with clarity, not apology.

**Light**

Soft, diffused, calibrated. The mesh-like surface structures we develop allow light to push through material in a way that feels warm rather than harsh. Designed to enrich a space rather than dominate it.

**Material Expression**

Tactile. Honest. The texture you see is the texture of making. Finishes support form rather than conceal it. Where wood and metal are introduced, they are there for structural and material reasons — not decoration.

**Scale**

From intimate table pieces to large-format pendant installations. The fabrication systems developed in-house allow dimensional flexibility that most production methods cannot offer.

---

## 7. What Makes Waves Different

**Customisation at minimal SKU.**
With computational design and digital fabrication, a lamp can be adapted — in scale, configuration, proportion — without retooling, without minimum order quantities, without the constraints of mass production. One-off or small batch, the process is the same.

**The maker has the technical depth.**
Custom G-code. Parametric modeling. Structural validation. These are not outsourced. The person who designs the object understands the machine that makes it. That connection — between design intent and fabrication logic — is visible in the result.

**Process transparency.**
If something took 19 hours to print, we say so. If a form went through eleven iterations before it felt right, that's part of the story. We don't disguise the making. We document it.
