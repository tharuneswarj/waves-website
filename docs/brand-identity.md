# Brand Identity & Usage Guidelines

*Last updated: March 2026*

---

## 1. Brand Overview

Waves Company is a design and manufacturing studio working at the intersection of computation, craft, and material honesty.

This document defines how our brand colours, typography, and logo should be used across digital and physical platforms to ensure consistency, clarity, and longevity.

The goal is not decoration.

The goal is disciplined expression.

---

## 2. Brand Colour Palette

### Primary — Deep Blue
**Hex:** #134686
**RGB:** 19, 70, 134

**Role:** Primary anchor colour

**Use for:**
- Website headers and footers
- Brand cover backgrounds
- Primary packaging sleeve
- Social grid base
- Hero sections
- Presentation slides

Blue represents structural confidence, intelligence, and architectural clarity.

---

### Primary — Cream
**Hex:** #FDF4E3
**RGB:** 253, 244, 227

**Role:** Primary neutral

**Use for:**
- Website background
- Packaging base
- Print collateral
- Product information cards
- Stationery
- Editorial layouts

Cream adds warmth and tactility. It supports material honesty and softness in tone.

---

### Secondary Accent — Coral
**Hex:** #ED3F27
**RGB:** 237, 63, 39

**Role:** Energy accent

**Use for:**
- CTA buttons
- Highlights
- Logo accent (secondary version)
- Stickers
- Callout text
- Microcopy emphasis

Coral adds personality and vibrancy without overpowering the system.

Use deliberately. Not everywhere.

---

### Micro Accent — Warm Yellow
**Hex:** #FEB21A
**RGB:** 254, 178, 26

**Role:** Micro highlight

**Use sparingly for:**
- Small icons
- Motion graphics
- Limited graphic emphasis
- Subtle detail

Yellow is a punctuation mark, not a paragraph.

---

## 3. Colour Usage Hierarchy

To maintain brand maturity and design discipline:

- **80%** → Blue + Cream
- **15%** → Coral
- **5%** → Yellow

Avoid using all four equally in a single layout.

Balance creates credibility.

## 4. Typography System

Waves uses the **DM type family** across all digital and print applications. Three weights of the same family create a coherent hierarchy — from editorial presence to body clarity to technical precision.

All three fonts are available free via Google Fonts.

---

### Display — DM Serif Display

**Use for:**
- Page titles and section headers
- Pull quotes and hero statements
- Editorial moments that need weight and character

**Character:** Architectural elegance. Confident without being decorative. The italic carries a quiet warmth.

**Usage note:** Use at larger sizes. Does not work well as body copy. Pair with DM Sans for body.

---

### Body & UI — DM Sans

**Use for:**
- Body copy across website, documents, and marketing
- Navigation and UI elements
- Subheadings and captions
- All functional text

**Character:** Warm but structured. Highly readable at small sizes. Reflects the brand's clarity without coldness.

**Weight guidance:**
- Light (300) — body copy, longer reads
- Regular (400) — UI, captions, standard copy
- Medium (500) — subheadings, emphasis, navigation

---

### Technical & Metadata — DM Mono

**Use for:**
- Technical specifications
- Product dimensions and material details
- Labels, metadata, timestamps
- Process documentation
- Packaging details (print time, iteration number)

**Character:** Precise. Code-adjacent. Signals the computational layer of the brand without performing it.

**Example uses:**
> `19h 42m print time`
> `Layer height: 0.16mm`
> `Iteration 07`

Use at small sizes. This is the detail layer — not the headline.

---

### Typography Hierarchy at a Glance

| Role | Font | Weight | Size guidance |
|---|---|---|---|
| Page title | DM Serif Display | Regular / Italic | 48px–80px |
| Section header | DM Sans | Medium 500 | 24px–36px |
| Body copy | DM Sans | Light 300 | 16px–18px |
| Caption / label | DM Sans | Regular 400 | 12px–14px |
| Technical detail | DM Mono | Light 300 | 10px–12px |

---

### What to Avoid in Typography

- Do not mix in other typefaces
- Do not use DM Serif Display for body text
- Do not use DM Mono for headlines
- Do not use all-caps in DM Serif Display
- Avoid tight letter-spacing on serif display text
- Avoid bold weights in DM Sans for body copy — light reads better at length

## 5. Logo Usage

### Primary Logo
**Line-based Wave Mark + Wordmark**

**Recommended backgrounds:**
- Deep Blue background with white/cream logo
- Cream background with blue logo

**Use for:**
- Website header
- Official documents
- Packaging base
- Press materials
- Large-format prints

This is the formal representation of Waves Company.

---

### Secondary Logo
**Solid Coral Wave + "waves company" wordmark**

**Use for:**
- Social media
- Stickers
- Merchandise
- Informal digital placements

This version carries more energy and should not replace the primary mark in formal contexts.

---

### Favicon / App Icon
**Simplified wave mark only**

Use where wordmark is too small to be legible.

---

## 6. Logo Placement Rules

- Maintain generous clear space around the logo
- Never stretch, distort, or skew the mark
- Do not apply gradients
- Do not add shadows or effects
- Do not rotate the logo
- Do not place on overly busy backgrounds
- Ensure strong contrast at all times

The logo should feel calm and grounded — not decorative.

---

## 7. Application Guidelines

### Website

- Cream background as primary base
- Blue for navigation, headers, structural elements
- Coral for buttons and key highlights
- Yellow only for small graphic cues
- DM Serif Display for hero titles
- DM Sans for all body and UI
- DM Mono for product specs and technical metadata

Layout should feel structured and breathable.

---

### Instagram

- Maintain consistent grid tone
- Alternate Blue and Cream backgrounds
- Coral used for emphasis
- Avoid high-saturation multi-colour posts
- DM Sans for text overlays
- DM Mono for process details and captions with technical content

Tone should feel curated, not chaotic.

---

### Packaging

**Primary format:**
- Cream matte box
- Blue or Coral sleeve
- Minimal text
- Subtle branding

**Inserts:**
- DM Mono for product details (print time, iteration, dimensions)
- DM Sans for the short copy line
- 1–2 lines maximum
- Subtle wit

Avoid overprinting.

---

### Studio Deck / Presentations

- Deep Blue cover with white logo
- Cream interior pages
- DM Serif Display for section openers
- DM Sans for body
- DM Mono for project specs, dimensions, and technical detail
- Coral used sparingly for key callouts

---

### Print & Collateral

- High contrast
- Generous margins
- Minimal layout
- No more than 2 brand colours per layout

Discipline over decoration.

---

## 8. Visual Expression Principles

All visual expression should reflect:

- Geometry and mathematical form
- Texture and material surface
- Light diffusion and shadow behaviour
- The making process — layers, toolpaths, iteration

**Photography should focus on:**
- Material detail and surface texture
- Shadow behaviour and light quality
- The object in a real, considered space
- Process — printing in progress, prototypes, material samples

**Avoid:**
- Lifestyle clutter
- Over-staging
- Over-filtered imagery
- Stock photography aesthetic

The image of the Ripple lamp on a wooden surface beside a book is the visual benchmark. That quality of light, restraint, and context is the standard.

---

## 9. Brand Consistency Checklist

Before publishing anything, confirm:

1. Does this layout follow colour hierarchy?
2. Is the correct logo version being used?
3. Is coral used as accent — not base?
4. Is yellow restrained?
5. Is the DM type system applied correctly?
6. Does this feel structured and calm?

If the design feels loud, it is not aligned.
